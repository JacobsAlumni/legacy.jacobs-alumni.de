Opportunity No. 1: 10 Years Alumni Reunions – 10% Bonanza!

Did you know that you can always end the Jacobs tuition postponement earlier than scheduled in the payback plan? And on your last payment – you typically got a 5% discount.

Now, imagine that you inherit some money, save up a few years after starting to work, win a jackpot, or more realistically – have friends or family who are willing to lend you the cash or can get access to a cheap bank loan (see Opportunity No. 3). In the past – you could use this to pay off your loan at Jacobs in one go and save 5% of the amount in the process.  

Now, here’s the best part, until the 31st of January 2015, the University is offering a one-time special 10 year reunion offer!

You get 10%, you get 10%, you all get 10% (sorry, we’re gifaholics – this wont stop)

What this means is – if you pay up your full Jacobs loan at once before the 31st of January, 2015 – you will get a solid 10% rebate on it.  However, check with your tax consultant on how to potentially split this across 2014 and 2015 in order to get maximum savings (see Opportunity 4 for more tax related ideas).

Opportunity No. 2: Larger Installments

As you know, Jacobs charges every year 7.75% interest on the amount that you owe the university. By paying back smaller amounts every month than what you could actually sustain and thereby stretching the payment period unnecessarily further, you are in fact hurting yourself. If you manage to pay back your tuition loan in one year, starting right after graduation, Jacobs will charge only once the 7.75%. In comparison, if you pay back everything only after 8 years, Jacobs will charge 7.75% eight times! Admittedly, each time the interest is charged on a smaller face value since you gradually reduce your outstanding debt, but the difference stays large.

By voluntarily increasing your monthly rate, you can therefore save a lot of cash!

Opportunity No. 3: Cover the Jacobs loan with a lower interest loan from a local bank (Refinancing)

The Jacobs interest rate reflects the credit risk the University is bearing. Jacobs has had a constant interest rate of 7.75% for everyone. At the time you came to Jacobs, there was no choice but to take that Jacobs tuition postponement. Now, however, you are likely to be in a different position. With a salary income and a positive banking track record over several years (including your account at beloved Sparkasse Bremen), there are now more opportunities to take out a loan from a “normal” bank than when you finished high school. Under the current market conditions interest rate levels are very low, so chances are high that you will find a loan that charges less than the Jacobs 7.75%. (Many German banks for example offer personal loans for as little as 4,5% these days).

Opportunity No. 4: Get a tax break

Costs that are incurred during a university education are, in many countries, tax deductable. What that means is that the tuition loan payments you are making each year can be substracted from the gross income that will be subject to income tax. That can be a lot of money you can save, especially in countries with high income tax. For Germany that means up to 6000 € that can be claimed for the Bachelor’s, and unlimited amounts for a Master and PhD every year. Just ask a fellow Alumni living in your country or any tax advisor to be sure.
